ComeAndBeJoyfulWithUs!

Our NEW meditative art program is specifically dedicated to expecting mothers, and aims to unite the world of a woman and her developing baby. The name of the program is ”The Joy of Co-Creativity”.

Why meditative art?

Meditative Art is a tool to bring one to the present moment, a practice of awareness and attentiveness, peacefulness and quietness. It is a way to connect to within - a 'higher self' or a higher power.

One approach is ego-based, where the sense of “I“ is placed firmly in the center. The “I” is expressing itself: “I create”, “I am the artist”, “I am healing myself through my art work”, etc.

In the second approach, the sense of “I” moves aside or disappears; what remains is a hollow vessel through which something higher or greater can be expressed. For a believer this can be God, for the naturalists it may be nature, or different colors and shapes that wish to be expressed. In this process the artist is humble and receptive. This is the focus of Meditative Art classes – making ourselves available to that which is perfect to be presented.

This meditative art program is based on integrity of visual art and music and focuses on the inner state of the artist. Music and art can have similar vibes, uniting the classical music of great composers such as Beethoven, Mozart and Vivaldi, with visual art, enhances the healing power of the creative process. Also, what does it mean that the program is focusing on the inner state of the artist? There is a reason why this program is using a meditative art approach. The simple explanation is that classical music and great pieces of art have high-energy vibes. With the help of classical music and art, the artist can come to the inner peace and harmony with oneself and develop a deeper, more meaningful relationship not only with oneself, but the baby as well.

The goals of the program are:

  • To create an atmosphere where women can experience the joy of harmony and co-creativity.
  • To feel as an equal participant in the process of the development of the future life.
  • Through the art and music, to start building inner connection between the mom and a baby with the goal of embracing this relationships in a deeper level after birth.
  • To experience the realization of inner creative potential through participation in art project.

Each of the lessons is enhanced with a unique combination of scientific research relating to health, genetics, brain development, as well as interesting stories and history behind the famous art and music masterpieces which we will explore. Each week, we will also explore a new theme to offer you a fresh perspective on pregnancy and creativity!

Each class includes design and development of an art project enhanced with musical composition. Each session will include different types of visual art (painting, sculpture, installation, collage) with implementation of Kazinik’s musical approach.

The program will have 12 sessions, each lasting 2 hours. The sessions will be held on a weekly basis.





ABOUT our Art Instructor, Valeria Kouznetsova

Valeria was born in Moscow, Russia and graduated from the Moscow Architectural Institute with a Bachelor Degree in Fine Arts and Architecture. In 1996 she moved to Canada and opened her own Art Studio, where she began teaching art classes to children and adults.

In 2012 Valeria received her Post-Baccalaureate Diploma in Applied Behaviour Analysis (Autism) and started to work with children with special needs. She also completed a specialized 8-week course, “The Art of Trauma”, which uses the basic principles and practice of art therapy and expressive arts to help clients overcome traumatic experiences through artistic expression.

Valeria completed Meditative Art Seminars in New York and Moscow, and frequently uses meditative art techniques in her work. Currently, Valeria teaches art at Victoria ballet Academy, Eureka Montessori school, Roots Russian school, Bee Smart Learning Center and Sick Kids Hospital. In 2016, she joined the "Art At Heart" no-profit organization, dedicated to bringing the creative arts to hospital patients.

As an artist, Valeria works with a variety of media, but, mostly with acrylic - her varied works were displayed at art shows. Valeria is always learning and experimenting with different art techniques and styles. She has studied traditional iconography with the Russian Orthodox Church in Toronto, trying to blend her fine artistic skills with the disciplined and humble work of iconography. She has also successfully completed Art Doll Making courses in Toronto and Moscow and started to give Art Doll Making classes to her students.

Why Meditative Art for Expecting Mothers?

Having worked with children for many years, sometimes, one encounters parental misconception and misunderstanding of the natural, simple creativity of a child. The expectations and conceptions of parents are often very limiting for their kids, and not very helpful for their harmonious growth. How interesting is the creative process of a child - it comes from the inner world of meditation and imagination and makes its way onto physical media through trial and error, which is meant to be a fun and exploratory process!!! So, if we, as parents, come into the picture, with no expectations, this could be tremendously helpful to our youngsters, creating the necessary "breathing space" for them. When a future mother gives herself permission to express freely through art with the aim of connecting with her baby, this is already a foundation for mutual freedom and understanding and self-exploration through art.

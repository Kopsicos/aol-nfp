Our 3rd annual youth-for-youth charitable event features performance by Interro Quartet, Arts Bazar, and Baking Contest for youth. Fun journey of music through the ages for adults and youth alike! With selections from Brahms & Beethoven

During intermission, we welcome youth to interact with the performers!

It's no coincidence that our charitable event is in the month of December, when everyone around the world is promoting the understanding of challenges and raising the awareness about every kind of people of disabilities.

The aim is to increase awareness about the daily life issues people with disabilities face with, their rights, needs for physical and virtual accessibility, and how can every single one of us help with inclusion and making life healthier.

“Youth-for-Youth” initiatives support Arts Heal Health programs at The Art of Life Community Health Centre, such as Mindfulness, Music and Art therapies, Community classes geared towards helping individuals and providing them with tools which allow healthier outlook to life.

The Interro Quartet

ERIC KIM-FUJITA
Violin

"When I play violin, the busy world around me fades into the background, and my focus becomes directed towards expressing myself with the language of music. I also enjoy sharing this with other people, especially my violin students who are just beginning their own musical journeys." Eric Kim-Fujita

STEVE KOH
Violin

Being a passionate performer and teacher, Steve Koh is currently a Sessional Lecturer at University of Toronto. Steve is interested in teaching and performing with fellow musicians who are dedicated to exploring the diverse aspects of art. This can be through researching obscure works of chamber music, or exploring the connections between jazz and classical music.

MAXIME DESPAX
Viola

Growing up in a musical family, I learned the incredible benefits of learning to play music, both as a soloist, and with my siblings. When we play music by ourselves, we are able to find a voice that can speak in ways that are impossible using words. As a member of an ensemble, we are able to use our unique musical voices to create something that can be powerful, heroic, subtle, or introspective, and every feeling and emotion in between. It is because of this, that I continue to play music.

SEBASTIAN OSTERTAG
Cello

"Being a musician is a way of not only connecting artistically with other players, but also a way of communicating with non musicians as well through music. I cherish all opportunities to play my cello, whether it is in a concert hall, prison yard, community centre or classroom, and am constantly surprised by the reactions that people have towards live music, regardless of age, upbringing, or previous artistic experiences."

Passion and Curiosity

Interro Quartet combines passionate performances with a curiosity to explore new sounds and venues in chamber music. The group’s repertoire includes works of classical music giants, as well as new compositions by emerging and established Canadian composers. Featuring award-winning graduates of University of Toronto, the Interro Quartet includes violinists Adam Despinic and Steve Koh, violist Maxime Despax, and cellist Sebastian Ostertag.

Mission

The mission of the Interro Quartet is not only to make quartet music accessible to those who seek it but also to broaden the spectrum of audiences through diverse programming. To complement this mission, members of the Interro Quartet are also avid educators and clinicians, dedicated to supporting education programs that encourage new ways for artists and listeners to share, experience, and incorporate chamber music into their daily lives.
